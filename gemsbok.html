GEMSBOK

The gemsbok, gemsbuk or South Africanoryx(Oryx gazella) is a large antelope in the genus Oryx.It is native to the arid regions of South Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. Some authorities formerly included the East African oryx as a subspecies.

The gemsbok is depicted on the coat of arms of Namibia, Where the current population of the species is estimated at 373,000 individuals.

The name "gemsbok", which itself is derived from Dutch name chamois, gemsbok, and further from German Gamse(chamois). Although some superficial similarities in appearance (especially in the facial pattern)are noticed, the chamois and the oryx are not closely related. The usual pronunciation in English is Gemsbok.

Gemsbok are HERBIVOROUS,The gemsbok is generally a grazer but changes to browsing during the dry season or when grass is sparse. It may dig up to a meter deep to find roots and tubers, supplementing its water intake by eating wild tsama melons and cucumbers, which can provide all the water required (3 liters per 100 kg bodyweight and day)

Gemsbok live in herds of about 10-40 animals, which consist of a dominant males, and females. They are maibly desert-dwelling and do not depend on drinking water to supply their physiological needs. They can reach running speeds of up to 60KM/h(37 mph).
